The value of each crystal is hidden from you until you click on it.

*** Thank you for visiting my Crystal Collector Game Page! In case this game has gotten you interested in crystal hunting IRL here are a few things to consider before you begin your adventure: ***

    Packing Right!
  • A Good Shovel - I have seen people using full size shovels but in my experience you can't beat a good study Mini Shovel. Trust me its worth the investment. Mining for crystals can involve tight spaces and steep hills.

  • A Bucket - A bucket, a backpack - whatever seems easiest and allows you to most easily manouver around possibly rough terrain. You can get super creative with this guys! - I like the backpack route. I try to consolidate my equipment so I have free hands as often as I can. Carrying a shovel a backpack with water and some snacks AND a bucket can be a little much. BUT whatever floats your boat. If you're going to go with the backpack I recommend lining it with a garbage can liner so you can easily have your crystals consolidated when you go to empty it out at the end of the day.

  • A Classifier Screen - A classifier screen can make a huge difference especially during (or after) a rainy day. Dirt can easily disguise crystals and screening out the dirt will make the hours you spend on your adventure way more effecient.

  • A Head Lamp - Not totally necessary - but why not. If your just getting into crystal hunting you may not immediately delve into 10ft holes in the mountain side to go digging. However, for safety precautions - like staying on the mountain for longer than anticipated, its better to be save than sorry if it gets dark out.

  • Gloves - And I mean the kind you get dirty. While gloves are not a necessity it can sometimes be uncomfrotable to have chunks of dirt underneath your nail beds. Gloves can also prevent cuts and scrapes - but now that I mention that bring some bandades and maybe a first aid kit too!

  • WATER WATER WATER! - I have gone on crystal excursions before where I've anticipated my journey being a couple hours at max. I pack a nalgene full of water and think that is enough but trust me when I say crystal hunting is addicting! Two hours turns into four when you begin finding various shapes and sizes of crystals in various spots and altitudes on the mountain.

  • Layers & Snacks - Like the comfort of a warm latte and fuzzy socks on a chilly day, an insulated jacket (or in the summer at least a rain jacket) and some GORP (Good Old Raisins and Peanuts) will relax, energize and rejuvinate all at the same time! FUEL YOURSELF!
      • Attitude
  • Respect - These little (and big) crystals, rocks etc are very sacred and should be treated as such.

  • Breathe - If something happens whether its an injury or a loss of direction in the area you're adventuring in, it is important to keep your cool and keep oxygen flowing to your brain. Taking a deep breath in and out a few times - s l o w l y - is essential to your mind, body connection and allows for a clear mind to make the right safe and careful decisions in these situations.

  • Lighten up! - Crystal hunting adventures are supposed to be a lighthearted, beautiful experience. Dont get discouraged if you dont find many - your just getting started and it takes some time for you to develop a keen eye for spotting them out. HAVE FUN!
